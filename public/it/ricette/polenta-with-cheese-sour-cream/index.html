Polenta with Cheese & Sour Cream

Mămăligă is the unwritten bread of Romanian cooking — a simple cornmeal porridge that fed the countryside from one end of the country to the other for centuries. In its version with cheese and sour cream, though, it becomes a dish in its own right: the warm, dense cornmeal catches salty sheep cheese and cool sour cream between its layers, a contrast of temperatures and flavours that lifts it well above the status of a side. It is eaten equally at a plain lunch and at the shepherd table, and a fried egg on top turns it into a full meal. Gluten-free by nature, it is one of the most honest recipes our kitchen has.

Ricetta tradizionale

Sapore autentico

Ingredienti 4 porzioni

  • 250g coarse cornmeal (polenta)
  • 1 litre water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 30g butter
  • 200g brânză de burduf or salty sheep cheese, crumbled
  • 200g full-fat sour cream
  • 4 eggs (optional, fried, to serve)

Come preparare

  1. 1Bring the water and salt to a boil in a heavy-bottomed pot.
  2. 2Pour in the cornmeal in a thin stream, whisking briskly so no lumps form.
  3. 3Lower the heat and cook for 15–20 minutes, stirring often with a wooden spoon, until the polenta pulls away from the sides of the pot and turns dense.
  4. 4Take it off the heat, stir in the butter and let it rest for two minutes.
  5. 5Serve it layered, alternating hot polenta with crumbled cheese and cold sour cream; for a full meal, top it with a fried egg with a soft yolk.
