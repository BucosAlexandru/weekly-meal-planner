Roasted Pork Loin

Roasted pork loin is the Sunday roast par excellence of Romanian cooking — a generous piece of meat, studded with garlic and rubbed with mustard and paprika, baked slowly alongside potatoes that soak up its juices. It is served hot, with garlic sauce (mujdei) and pickles, but it is just as good cold the next day, thinly sliced on the holiday appetiser platter or in sandwiches. It is the dish a family gathers around, simple to make yet impressive on the platter. The key is patience: covered at first to stay tender, uncovered at the end to take on a crust, with a short rest before slicing so it stays juicy down to the last cut.

असली स्वाद

सामग्री 6 सर्विंग्स

  • 1.2kg pork loin
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 1 carrot, cut into batons (for larding)
  • 2 tbsp mustard
  • 1 tsp sweet paprika
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 3 tbsp oil
  • 150ml dry white wine
  • 800g potatoes, quartered
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

बनाने का तरीका

  1. 1Make deep cuts in the loin and stud it with slivers of garlic and batons of carrot.
  2. 2Mix the mustard with the oil, paprika, thyme, salt and pepper and rub the meat all over with the paste; let it marinate for at least an hour, ideally overnight.
  3. 3Sear the loin on all sides in a hot pan, then set it in a roasting tin, pour in the wine, add the bay leaves and arrange the quartered potatoes around it.
  4. 4Cover the tin with foil and roast at 180°C for about an hour, basting now and then with the juices, so it stays juicy.
  5. 5Take off the foil for the last 15–20 minutes, to brown nicely and form a crust.
  6. 6Let the meat rest for 10 minutes before slicing, so the juices stay inside.
