Gazpacho

Gazpacho is the signature cold soup of Andalusia in southern Spain, born in the sun-scorched farmhouses of the Guadalquivir valley where field workers needed something cooling, nourishing, and simple. What makes it remarkable is method, not recipe: ripe summer tomatoes blended with day-old bread, olive oil emulsified in a thin stream, then chilled to 6–8°C so the flavours intensify and the texture turns silky. It should never be served at room temperature — a good gazpacho tastes of Spain in August.

Ingredients 4 servings

  • 800g ripe tomatoes (San Marzano or plum), roughly chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper (about 200g), deseeded and chopped
  • 1 medium cucumber (about 200g), peeled and chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 100g day-old white bread, crusts removed
  • 60ml extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp fine salt
  • 200ml cold water

How to make it

  1. 1Tear the bread into pieces and soak in 200ml cold water for 10 minutes.
  2. 2Put tomatoes, pepper, cucumber, and garlic into a blender and blitz for 1 minute on high.
  3. 3Add the soaked bread along with its soaking water, vinegar, and salt; blitz again.
  4. 4With the motor running, pour olive oil in a thin stream — this emulsification gives the soup its silky texture.
  5. 5Taste and adjust salt and vinegar.
  6. 6Pass through a fine-mesh sieve for a smoother result, or leave it rustic.
  7. 7Chill at least 2 hours — gazpacho at 6–8°C is vastly more flavourful than at room temperature.
  8. 8Serve in chilled bowls with a drizzle of olive oil on top.
💡
Tip: Taste before serving and adjust seasoning and salt to your liking.
