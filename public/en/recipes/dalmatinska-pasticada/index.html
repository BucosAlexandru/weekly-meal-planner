Dalmatinska Pasticada

Dalmatinska pašticada is the signature celebration dish of coastal Croatia, served at weddings, baptisms, and major family occasions across Dalmatia. The recipe is centuries old and appears in Venetian-influenced Dalmatian cookbooks from the eighteenth century, reflecting the long period of Venetian rule over the Dalmatian coast. The preparation is a two-day process. The beef is larded — garlic and bacon are physically inserted into the meat with a knife — then marinated overnight in red wine vinegar. This acidic bath tenderizes the muscle fibres and flavours the interior. The long braise with red wine, prunes, and tomatoes produces a sauce that is sweet-sour and dense: the prunes dissolve completely, and the braising liquid reduces to a dark, complex glaze. Gnocchi — njoki in Croatian — are the traditional accompaniment.

Ingredients 4 servings

  • 1 kg beef rump or topside, in one piece
  • 4 garlic cloves, cut into thin slivers
  • 100 g smoked bacon or pancetta, cut into small lardons
  • 3 medium onions (about 450 g), roughly chopped
  • 2 medium carrots (about 200 g), roughly chopped
  • 2 celery stalks (about 100 g), roughly chopped
  • 200 ml red wine vinegar
  • 300 ml dry red wine
  • 400 g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 100 g pitted prunes, roughly chopped
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

How to make it

  1. 1Use a sharp knife to cut deep incisions all over the beef and push the garlic slivers and bacon lardons into the holes.
  2. 2Place the beef in a glass or ceramic bowl with the vinegar, 1 of the chopped onions, 1 carrot, 1 celery stalk, and 1 bay leaf; cover and refrigerate for 12-24 hours, turning the beef a few times.
  3. 3Remove the beef from the marinade, pat completely dry with paper towels, and season with salt and pepper; discard the marinade and vegetables.
  4. 4Heat the oil in a large, heavy pot over high heat and sear the beef on all sides for 8-10 minutes until deeply browned.
  5. 5Add the remaining onions, carrots, and celery and cook for 5 minutes until softened.
  6. 6Pour in the wine and let it boil vigorously for 2-3 minutes, scraping the bottom, then add the tomatoes, prunes, and remaining bay leaf.
  7. 7Cover tightly and cook over very low heat for 2.5 to 3 hours, turning the beef every 30-40 minutes, until a knife meets no resistance.
  8. 8Remove the beef, slice thickly, return to the sauce, adjust seasoning, and serve with gnocchi, pasta, or polenta.
💡
Tip: Let the meat rest for 5 minutes after cooking before slicing or serving.
