Pepian

Pepian is a Guatemalan national sauce with pre-Columbian Maya roots — a rich blend of toasted sesame and pumpkin seeds, dried chilies, tomatoes, and spices ground into a thick paste in which chicken or turkey pieces are cooked. The dish exists in red, green, and black variants, each with distinct flavor profiles. Pepian is served with white rice, potatoes, and tortillas. The recipe is passed down through generations and is considered one of Guatemala's four national dishes. At Maya festivals and ceremonies, the communal preparation of pepian is part of the social ritual.

Ingredients 4 servings

  • 1.2 kg bone-in chicken thighs and drumsticks (about 8 pieces)
  • 1.5 L cold water + 1 tsp salt for poaching
  • 100 g raw hulled pumpkin seeds (pepitas)
  • 50 g sesame seeds
  • 4 dried guajillo chilies, stemmed and deseeded
  • 2 dried ancho or pasilla chilies, stemmed and deseeded
  • 1 dried chipotle chili (optional, for smokiness)
  • 4 medium tomatoes (~500 g), halved
  • 4 tomatillos (~200 g), husked and halved
  • 1 medium onion (~150 g), quartered
  • 5 unpeeled garlic cloves
  • 1 Ceylon cinnamon stick (~5 cm)
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper + 0.5 tsp ground allspice
  • 1 corn tortilla, torn into pieces (for thickening)
  • 200 g güisquil (chayote squash) or zucchini, peeled and cubed 2 cm
  • 300 g potatoes, peeled and cubed 2 cm
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil for cooking the paste
  • 2 tbsp fresh cilantro, finely chopped, to garnish
  • Salt to taste
  • 500 g cooked white rice + warm tortillas to serve

How to make it

  1. 1POACH THE CHICKEN: place 1.2 kg chicken pieces in 1.5 L cold water with 1 tsp salt in a large pot.
  2. 2Bring to a boil, skim the foam, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
  3. 3Cook 25–30 minutes until the chicken is just cooked through (juices run clear).
  4. 4Remove the chicken to a plate and reserve the broth (~1.2 L).
  5. 5TOAST THE SEEDS: in a dry skillet over medium heat, toast 100 g pumpkin seeds, stirring constantly, 3–4 minutes until they puff up and turn golden brown — do not burn.
  6. 6Transfer to a bowl.
  7. 7In the same skillet, toast 50 g sesame seeds 1–2 minutes until golden and fragrant, stirring constantly.
  8. 8Add to the same bowl.
  9. 9CHAR THE VEGETABLES: in a dry skillet or comal over medium-high heat, char 4 halved tomatoes (cut side down first), 4 halved tomatillos, 1 quartered onion, and 5 unpeeled garlic cloves.
  10. 10Turn occasionally for 8–10 minutes until the skins are blackened in spots and the vegetables soften.
  11. 11Peel the garlic after they cool.
  12. 12TOAST THE CHILIES AND SPICES: in the same skillet, briefly toast 4 guajillo + 2 ancho + 1 chipotle (optional) chilies, pressing them flat with a spatula, 20–30 seconds per side until fragrant — do not burn.
  13. 13Transfer to a bowl and cover with boiling water; soak 15 minutes.
  14. 14In the same skillet, briefly toast 1 cinnamon stick, 2 cloves, 1 tsp pepper, and 0.5 tsp allspice for 30 seconds.
  15. 15Toast the torn tortilla pieces 30 seconds per side until crisp.
  16. 16BLEND THE PASTE: in a blender, combine the drained chilies, the charred vegetables, peeled garlic, toasted seeds, toasted tortilla pieces, and 500 ml of the chicken broth.
  17. 17Blend on high 3–4 minutes until the paste is completely smooth — strain through a fine sieve if you want it silky.
  18. 18COOK THE SAUCE: heat 2 tablespoons of oil in the pot over medium heat.
  19. 19Pour in the blended paste — be careful, it will splutter.
  20. 20Simmer 10 minutes, stirring frequently with a wooden spoon, until the sauce darkens and the rawness cooks off.
  21. 21The sauce should coat the back of the spoon.
  22. 22STEW THE CHICKEN: return the chicken pieces to the pot, then pour in the remaining 700 ml broth + 200 g chayote + 300 g potato cubes.
  23. 23Simmer gently, partially covered, 25–30 minutes until the potatoes and chayote are fork-tender and the chicken is fully infused with the sauce.
  24. 24Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  25. 25Adjust salt to taste — the sauce should taste deeply spiced and slightly sweet.
  26. 26SERVE: spoon the pepian into deep plates over a bed of steamed white rice.
  27. 27Top each serving with a sprinkle of fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime (optional).
  28. 28Serve with warm corn tortillas on the side for scooping up the sauce.
💡
Tip: Let the meat rest for 5 minutes after cooking before slicing or serving.
