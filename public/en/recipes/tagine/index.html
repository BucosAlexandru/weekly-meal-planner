Tagine

The tagine is both the conical clay pot of the Berbers and the slow-cooked dish that bubbles inside it — lamb or chicken melting with preserved lemon, green olives, turmeric and cinnamon, the smell of Marrakech around the table.

Ingredients 6 servings

  • 800 g lamb or chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 preserved lemon, sliced
  • 100 g green olives
  • Coriander, parsley, olive oil, salt

How to make it

  1. 1Sauté onion and garlic in oil in a tagine or heavy pot, add the spices and ginger, toast 1 minute.
  2. 2Add the meat and brown well.
  3. 3Pour in 200 ml water, add preserved lemon slices and olives.
  4. 4Cover tightly and simmer on low for 90 minutes (chicken) or 2 hours (lamb).
  5. 5Scatter coriander, serve with couscous or bread.
💡
Tip: Keep the lid on as much as possible — the conical shape traps steam and returns moisture to the pot. Every lift dries the dish out.
