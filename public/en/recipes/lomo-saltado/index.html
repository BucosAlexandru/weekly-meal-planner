Lomo Saltado

Lomo saltado is one of Peru's most emblematic stir-fries, born from the chifa tradition — the fusion of Cantonese cooking with Peruvian ingredients that emerged in the nineteenth century through Chinese immigrant communities along the Pacific coast. Strips of beef sirloin are sautéed over extreme heat with red onion, ají amarillo, and tomatoes, then doused with soy sauce and a splash of vinegar. The dish arrives with two starches: crispy french fries folded directly into the wok alongside the meat, and a separate mound of white rice. The combination of Chinese technique, Peruvian peppers, and the dual carbohydrate is entirely its own thing — a fusion that no longer feels like fusion.

Ingredients 4 servings

  • 600g beef sirloin or tenderloin, cut into 1 cm strips
  • 3 large potatoes (about 700g), cut into thick fries
  • 1 large red onion, cut into 1 cm-thick wedges
  • 2 medium Roma tomatoes, cut into wedges (or thick slices)
  • 1 yellow ají amarillo pepper (or 1 red bell pepper + 1 tsp ají amarillo paste)
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 30g fresh coriander, roughly chopped
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil (plus 500ml for frying potatoes)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 400g white basmati rice, cooked
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges (optional)

How to make it

  1. 1Season the beef strips with salt and pepper.
  2. 2Let them rest for 10 minutes at room temperature — they will sear better at high heat.
  3. 3Heat 500ml of oil in a deep pan to 175°C.
  4. 4Fry the potatoes for 6–7 minutes until golden and crisp.
  5. 5Drain on a rack and salt lightly while hot.
  6. 6Heat a large wok or cast-iron pan over very high heat with 2 tbsp of vegetable oil — the oil should just begin to smoke.
  7. 7This is the critical step: the pan must be very hot for the Peruvian "wok hei" effect.
  8. 8Add the beef in a single layer.
  9. 9Leave untouched for 30 seconds to sear, then stir-fry vigorously for 90 seconds — the beef should be browned outside but still pink in the centre.
  10. 10Lift onto a plate.
  11. 11In the same wok, add 1 tbsp more oil.
  12. 12Stir-fry the onion wedges and ají amarillo for 90 seconds — the onion should stay crisp, not soft.
  13. 13Add the garlic and ginger, stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  14. 14Add the tomatoes and cook for 1 minute — they should soften slightly but not break down.
  15. 15Pour the soy sauce and red wine vinegar around the edge of the pan (they will steam and create aromatic smoke).
  16. 16Return the beef with its juices and add the fries.
  17. 17Toss vigorously for 30 seconds, until everything is coated.
  18. 18Turn off the heat, scatter the chopped coriander, and toss once.
  19. 19Serve immediately over warm basmati rice with lime wedges on the side.
💡
Tip: Let the meat rest for 5 minutes after cooking before slicing or serving.
