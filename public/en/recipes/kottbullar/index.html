Kottbullar

Swedish meatballs — köttbullar — are one of Sweden's most internationally recognised dishes, spread globally in large part through the influence of IKEA. At home they have been a staple of everyday cooking and festive smörgåsbord alike for over a century. The mixture of pork and beef, softened with soaked breadcrumbs and a touch of cream, produces meatballs notably lighter than their southern European counterparts. The defining accompaniment is a smooth cream-based pan gravy made from the drippings, served alongside buttery mashed potatoes, lingonberry jam, and briefly pickled cucumber. Every element balances the others: the savoury meatball, the sweet-tart jam, the creamy sauce.

Ingredients 4 servings

  • 400g ground beef
  • 200g ground pork
  • 1 small onion, very finely chopped
  • 80g dried breadcrumbs
  • 120ml milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 tsp ground allspice
  • 1/2 tsp freshly grated nutmeg
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp white pepper
  • 40g butter + 1 tbsp oil (for frying)
  • For the sauce:
  • 30g butter
  • 30g flour
  • 400ml beef stock
  • 200ml double cream (heavy cream)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce (for deep colour)
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • To serve:
  • Mashed potatoes
  • Lingonberry jam (or cranberry sauce)
  • Pickled cucumber

How to make it

  1. 1Soak the breadcrumbs in milk in a small bowl for 5 minutes.
  2. 2This "panada" is the secret to tender Swedish meatballs.
  3. 3Sauté the very finely chopped onion in 10g butter for 6–8 minutes over medium heat, until soft and translucent without colouring.
  4. 4Cool completely.
  5. 5In a large bowl, combine the ground beef and pork, soaked breadcrumbs, cooled onion, egg, allspice, nutmeg, salt, and white pepper.
  6. 6Knead by hand for 2 minutes, until the mixture is uniform and sticky.
  7. 7With wet palms, shape into 2.5 cm balls (walnut-sized) — you'll get about 28–32 meatballs.
  8. 8Heat the rest of the butter and oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  9. 9Fry the meatballs in batches of 8–10, turning often, for 8 minutes, until golden brown all over.
  10. 10Lift onto a plate.
  11. 11For the sauce: in the same pan (with the leftover juices), melt 30g butter and whisk in the flour.
  12. 12Cook 1 minute to form a roux.
  13. 13Pour in the beef stock gradually, whisking constantly to avoid lumps.
  14. 14Add the cream, soy sauce, and mustard.
  15. 15Simmer 3–4 minutes until thickened and silky.
  16. 16Return the meatballs to the sauce and cook 5 more minutes on low heat, so they soften and absorb the flavours.
  17. 17Serve over mashed potatoes with lingonberry jam and pickled cucumber alongside.
💡
Tip: Let the meat rest for 5 minutes after cooking before slicing or serving.
