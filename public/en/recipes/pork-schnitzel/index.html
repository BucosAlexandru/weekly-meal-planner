Pork schnitzel

The classic Wiener Schnitzel (veal) sits alongside its more accessible pork sibling — pounded thin, breaded in golden crumbs and shallow-fried in butter, with a wedge of lemon on the side. Central-European canteen food but also a Sunday lunch.

Ingredients 4 servings

  • 4 boneless pork loin chops, pounded thin
  • 2 eggs
  • 100 g flour
  • 150 g breadcrumbs
  • Butter and oil for frying
  • Salt, pepper
  • 1 lemon, sliced

How to make it

  1. 1Season the chops with salt and pepper.
  2. 2Dredge in flour, then beaten egg, then breadcrumbs, pressing well to coat.
  3. 3Melt butter with a splash of oil in a wide pan, fry 3-4 minutes per side until golden and crisp.
  4. 4Drain on paper.
  5. 5Serve immediately with lemon wedges.
