Spaghetti Carbonara

Ingredients 4 servings

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 200g guanciale (or pancetta), cut into 1cm cubes
  • 4 large egg yolks + 1 whole egg
  • 80g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
  • 40g Parmigiano Reggiano, finely grated
  • 1 tsp freshly cracked black pepper
  • Fine sea salt for pasta water

How to make it

  1. 1Bring 4 litres of well-salted water to a rolling boil; cook spaghetti until 2 minutes before al dente.
  2. 2Render guanciale in a cold pan over medium-low heat for 8–10 minutes until the fat turns golden and the edges crisp; remove from heat.
  3. 3Whisk 4 egg yolks and 1 whole egg with Pecorino, Parmigiano, and cracked pepper into a thick cream.
  4. 4Reserve 200ml of starchy pasta water before draining.
  5. 5Add the hot spaghetti to the guanciale pan off the heat, toss well, then pour in the egg cream and add pasta water a splash at a time — 2–3 splashes — tossing vigorously until every strand is coated in a glossy, silky sauce; residual heat sets the egg without scrambling it.
  6. 6Serve immediately in warm bowls with extra Pecorino and pepper..
💡
Tip: Add a splash of pasta cooking water to the sauce for a creamier texture.
