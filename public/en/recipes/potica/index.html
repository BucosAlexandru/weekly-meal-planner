Potica

Potica is Slovenia's national pastry — a leavened roll wrapped around a generous layer of ground walnut with honey, egg yolks, and butter, baked in a tubular mold. The dough is rich, almost brioche-like, and the walnut filling is dense and fragrant. The visible spiral when cut is the pastry's signature. Potica is baked at Christmas, Easter, baptisms, and weddings — its absence from the Slovenian festive table would be unthinkable. Recipes differ from family to family, with hundreds of regional variants (with poppy seed, wild mushroom, ginger). Potice is the plural, because more than one is always baked at a time.

Ingredients 8 servings

  • For the dough: 500 g bread flour (or strong all-purpose), plus extra for kneading
  • 7 g instant dry yeast OR 21 g fresh yeast
  • 50 g caster sugar (1 tsp to feed the yeast, the rest in the dough)
  • 5 g fine salt
  • 250 ml warm whole milk (38 °C)
  • 100 g unsalted butter, melted and cooled to lukewarm
  • 2 large egg yolks + 1 whole egg for the dough
  • 1 tbsp dark rum (optional, traditional)
  • Finely grated zest of 1 unwaxed lemon
  • For the walnut filling: 400 g finely ground shelled walnuts (freshly ground — pre-ground will not work)
  • 200 ml hot whole milk
  • 150 g acacia honey (or mild floral honey)
  • 100 g unsalted butter, melted, for the filling
  • 100 g caster sugar for the filling
  • 2 large egg yolks + 2 egg whites (whipped to stiff peaks separately)
  • 1 tbsp dark rum, 1 tsp ground cinnamon, 0.5 tsp ground cloves, 1 tbsp fresh breadcrumbs
  • For finishing: 1 egg beaten with 1 tbsp milk (egg wash), icing sugar to dust after baking

How to make it

  1. 1ACTIVATE THE YEAST: in a small bowl, dissolve 7 g instant dry yeast (or 21 g fresh yeast) into 250 ml warm milk (~38 °C) with 1 tsp sugar.
  2. 2Let stand 10 minutes until a creamy foam appears on top — if it doesn't foam, the yeast is dead, start over.
  3. 3THE DOUGH: in a stand mixer with dough hook (or by hand), combine 500 g flour with the remaining 47 g sugar, 5 g salt, and lemon zest.
  4. 4Pour in the foamed yeast-milk, 2 egg yolks + 1 whole egg, 100 g melted-and-cooled butter, and 1 tbsp rum.
  5. 5Knead on medium speed 10 minutes (or 15 by hand) until the dough is smooth, elastic, and pulls cleanly from the bowl — it should be soft but not sticky.
  6. 6Cover with film and proof in a warm place 1.5–2 hours until doubled.
  7. 7THE WALNUT FILLING: place 400 g freshly ground walnuts in a large bowl.
  8. 8Pour over 200 ml hot milk, 100 g melted butter, 150 g honey, and 100 g sugar.
  9. 9Stir well to scald the walnuts and dissolve the sugars.
  10. 10Cool 10 minutes, then mix in 2 egg yolks, 1 tbsp rum, 1 tsp cinnamon, 0.5 tsp cloves, 1 tbsp breadcrumbs, and a pinch of salt.
  11. 11In a separate bowl, whip 2 egg whites to stiff peaks, then fold them gently into the walnut mixture in 3 additions — the filling should be airy and spreadable.
  12. 12ROLL THE DOUGH: lightly flour a clean tea towel and turn the proofed dough out onto it.
  13. 13Punch down gently, then roll out into a thin rectangle 60 × 40 cm and 4–5 mm thick — work from the centre outward, lifting and rotating to prevent sticking.
  14. 14The dough must be thin enough that the spiral is visible after baking, but thick enough to hold the filling.
  15. 15SPREAD THE FILLING: spread the walnut filling evenly over the rolled dough, leaving a 2 cm border along the long edges.
  16. 16Pat it gently with a spatula — do not overload, the filling should be 5–6 mm thick.
  17. 17Sprinkle a few extra ground walnuts if any spot oozes thin.
  18. 18ROLL UP: using the tea towel to help, roll up the dough tightly from the long side, forming a long log.
  19. 19Roll snug but not so tight you compress the filling.
  20. 20Pinch the seam closed and tuck in the ends.
  21. 21The log should be about 60 cm long.
  22. 22SHAPE IN MOLD + SECOND PROOF: grease a tubular potica mold (or a 24 cm Bundt pan or a kugelhopf form) thoroughly with butter and dust with flour.
  23. 23Lay the log in the mold, seam side down, joining the ends to form a closed ring (pinch them together).
  24. 24Cover loosely and proof again in a warm place 45–60 minutes until the dough has risen visibly to almost fill the mold.
  25. 25Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 175 °C.
  26. 26BAKE: brush the top with the egg wash.
  27. 27Bake on the middle rack of the 175 °C oven 45–55 minutes — cover loosely with foil after 30 min if the top is browning too fast.
  28. 28The potica is done when a skewer comes out clean and the internal temperature reaches 92–95 °C.
  29. 29Let it rest 15 minutes in the mold, then carefully invert onto a wire rack to cool completely.
  30. 30Dust generously with icing sugar before slicing.
  31. 31Serve at room temperature with strong coffee or tea.
💡
Tip: Let the dessert cool completely before serving for the best texture and flavour.
