Machboos

Machboos — considered Kuwait's national dish — is an aromatic rice pilaf with meat (lamb, chicken, fish, or shrimp) cooked with a complex spice blend called baharat: cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, allspice, cumin, turmeric, and sometimes loomi (dried lime). The meat is first fried with onion and spices, water is added to create a concentrated stock, and the rice absorbs this stock during the final cooking stage. Served with deeba (tamarind and sugar sauce) and onion salad. Fish versions (samak machboos) are especially prized along the coast. Preparation takes several hours and is the heart of Gulf family banquets.

Ingredients 4 servings

  • 1.5 kg bone-in skin-on chicken thighs and drumsticks (or 1.2 kg bone-in lamb shoulder cut into 6 cm chunks)
  • 400 g basmati rice, rinsed and soaked in cold water for 30 minutes, then drained
  • 60 ml neutral oil (or 30 g ghee + 2 tbsp oil for a richer flavour)
  • 3 large onions (about 600 g), finely sliced — reserve a small handful raw for serving
  • 6 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of fresh ginger (about 30 g), finely grated
  • 3 medium tomatoes (about 300 g), grated (or 200 g canned chopped tomatoes)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 dried limes (loomi), pierced with a knife in 3 places (or 2 tbsp fresh lime juice as a less-traditional substitute)
  • 1 tbsp baharat (or 1 tsp ground cinnamon + 1 tsp ground cumin + 1 tsp ground coriander + 0.5 tsp ground cardamom + 0.5 tsp ground black pepper)
  • 0.5 tsp ground turmeric
  • 4 whole green cardamom pods, lightly cracked + 1 cinnamon stick (5 cm) + 4 whole cloves + 2 bay leaves
  • 1 small bunch fresh coriander (about 30 g), stems chopped fine for the pot, leaves reserved for garnish
  • 2 fresh green chillies, slit lengthwise
  • 1.2 litres hot water (or unsalted chicken stock for more depth)
  • 1.5 tsp fine sea salt, plus more to taste
  • 60 g flaked almonds and 40 g sultanas, lightly toasted in 1 tsp ghee (to scatter on top)
  • 0.5 tsp saffron threads soaked in 2 tbsp warm water (optional, for finishing the rice)

How to make it

  1. 1Rinse the basmati rice under cold running water until the water runs almost clear, then soak in fresh cold water for 30 minutes.
  2. 2This removes excess starch and shortens the final cooking step.
  3. 3Drain just before use.
  4. 4Pat the chicken pieces dry and season with 0.5 tsp of the salt.
  5. 5Heat the oil (or ghee + oil) in a heavy 28 cm pot over medium-high heat.
  6. 6When shimmering, add the chicken skin-side down and sear for 4–5 minutes per side until deep golden.
  7. 7Lift onto a plate.
  8. 8Reduce the heat to medium.
  9. 9Add the sliced onions and a pinch of salt to the same pot and cook for 12–15 minutes, stirring often, until deeply golden and very soft.
  10. 10Add the garlic and ginger and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  11. 11Stir in the cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, cloves, bay leaves, baharat, and turmeric.
  12. 12Toast for 30 seconds — the spices should turn aromatic and deepen in colour.
  13. 13Add the tomato paste and grated tomatoes and cook, stirring, for 4–5 minutes, until the mixture darkens to a rich red and the oil splits out around the edges.
  14. 14Return the chicken to the pot in a single layer.
  15. 15Pour in the hot water (or stock), add the dried limes, slit chillies, chopped coriander stems, and remaining salt.
  16. 16Bring to a gentle boil, then cover, reduce to the lowest simmer, and cook for 35–40 minutes (chicken) or 1 h 30 min (lamb), until the meat is fork-tender and the broth is deeply flavoured.
  17. 17Lift the chicken onto a plate and cover loosely with foil to keep warm.
  18. 18Skim any excess fat from the broth.
  19. 19Add the drained rice to the pot, gently pushing it down so it is just covered by the broth (top up with hot water if needed — you want about 2.5 cm of liquid above the rice).
  20. 20Bring back to a gentle boil, then cover with a tight lid, reduce to the lowest possible heat, and cook undisturbed for 18 minutes.
  21. 21Turn off the heat.
  22. 22Drizzle the saffron water (if using) over the rice, place the chicken pieces on top, replace the lid, and rest for 10 minutes — the steam finishes cooking the chicken and the rice grains separate.
  23. 23Fluff the rice with a fork, transfer to a wide platter with the chicken on top, and scatter with toasted almonds, sultanas, raw onion slices, and fresh coriander leaves.
  24. 24Serve with deeba (tamarind sauce) or yoghurt on the side.
💡
Tip: Let the meat rest for 5 minutes after cooking before slicing or serving.
