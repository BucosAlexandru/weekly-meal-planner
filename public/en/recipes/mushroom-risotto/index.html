Mushroom risotto

Mushroom risotto is the damp wisdom of Lombardy and Piedmont — Arborio or Carnaroli rice biting al dente, bathed in stock and butter with Parmigiano, scented with autumn porcini foraged from the northern woods.

Ingredients 6 servings

  • 300 g Arborio or Carnaroli rice
  • 400 g mixed mushrooms (porcini, button)
  • 1 onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 100 ml dry white wine
  • 1 L hot vegetable stock
  • 80 g Parmigiano Reggiano, grated
  • 50 g butter
  • Olive oil, parsley, salt, pepper

How to make it

  1. 1Sauté onion and garlic in oil, add sliced mushrooms and brown for 5 minutes.
  2. 2Add the rice and toast 1 minute until translucent.
  3. 3Deglaze with white wine, let evaporate.
  4. 4Add hot stock one ladle at a time, stirring often, until the rice is al dente — 18 minutes.
  5. 5Off the heat, beat in butter and Parmigiano.
  6. 6Rest 2 minutes for the mantecatura.
  7. 7Parsley, serve immediately.
💡
Tip: Add stock a ladle at a time and stir often enough to release starch but not constantly; the rice's own starch is what makes the sauce.
