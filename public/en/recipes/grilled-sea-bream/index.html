Grilled Sea Bream

Grilled sea bream is the soul of Mediterranean tavernas from Greece to Cyprus — a whole white-fleshed fish, simply salted, stuffed with lemon and herbs, charred on coals until the skin crackles and the flesh slips off the bone.

Total time35m

Ingredients 4 servings

  • 2 whole sea bream (about 500 g each)
  • 2 lemons
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • Fresh dill and parsley
  • Thyme
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Coarse salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper

How to make it

  1. 1Scale and gut the sea bream, make 3 slashes on each side.
  2. 2Salt inside and out.
  3. 3Stuff the belly with lemon slices, sliced garlic, dill, parsley and thyme.
  4. 4Drizzle with olive oil.
  5. 5Grill over hot coals 6-8 minutes per side until the flesh lifts off the bone.
  6. 6Serve with extra lemon wedges and a Greek salad alongside.
