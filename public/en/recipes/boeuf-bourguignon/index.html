Boeuf Bourguignon

Boeuf Bourguignon is the gastronomic jewel of the Burgundy region of France, a dish that transforms beef into liquid velvet through long hours of simmering in red wine. The recipe traces back to French rural kitchens, where cooks used it to tenderize tougher cuts. Each pot develops its own character through the garnish of carrots, mushrooms, and pearl onions that absorb the essence of the wine. It is a masterpiece of culinary patience.

Ingredients 6 servings

  • 1.5 kg beef chuck or shin, cubed 4 cm (some marbling for richness)
  • 200 g lardons (smoked pork belly cut 1 cm × 3 cm) OR 200 g thick-cut bacon diced
  • 750 ml full-bodied Burgundy red wine (Pinot Noir) — DO NOT use cheap wine
  • 200 ml beef stock
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 large onion (~250 g), finely diced for the marinade and braise
  • 2 medium carrots (~250 g), cut into 2 cm chunks
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 30 g unsalted butter + 2 tbsp olive oil for searing
  • 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 bouquet garni: 3 thyme sprigs + 2 bay leaves + 6 parsley stems tied with kitchen string
  • 250 g pearl onions (silverskin), peeled
  • 250 g cremini or button mushrooms, halved or quartered
  • 30 g extra butter for the mushroom-and-pearl-onion garnish
  • 1.5 tsp salt + 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp Cognac OR brandy for optional flambé
  • Fresh flat-leaf parsley + baguette + boiled new potatoes or buttered egg noodles to serve

How to make it

  1. 1MARINATE THE BEEF (12–24 h ahead, Julia Child's non-negotiable): place 1.5 kg cubed beef in a non-reactive bowl with 750 ml red Burgundy + 1 chopped onion + 1 chopped carrot + 4 garlic + the bouquet garni + 1 tsp pepper.
  2. 2Cover and refrigerate 12–24 hours.
  3. 3Drain through a sieve, RESERVING the wine.
  4. 4Pat the beef VERY DRY with paper towel — wet meat steams, never sears.
  5. 5RENDER LARDONS: in a heavy Dutch oven over medium heat, cook 200 g lardons 6–8 min until crisp and golden, the fat fully rendered.
  6. 6Lift them out with a slotted spoon onto a plate.
  7. 7Leave 2 tbsp fat in the pot.
  8. 8SEAR THE BEEF: raise heat to high.
  9. 9Add 1 tbsp olive oil + 15 g butter.
  10. 10Working in 3–4 batches without crowding, sear the dried beef cubes 2–3 min per side until deeply browned on all sides — about 8 min per batch.
  11. 11Transfer each batch to a tray.
  12. 12The dark fond stuck to the pot bottom is the flavour base — leave it.
  13. 13SOFFRITTO + FLAMBÉ: reduce heat to medium.
  14. 14Add 250 g diced onion + 250 g carrot chunks to the pot.
  15. 15Cook 5 min until starting to soften.
  16. 16Stir in 4 minced garlic + 2 tbsp tomato paste and cook 1 min.
  17. 17Pour in 2 tbsp Cognac and ignite carefully (the flames die in 30 s).
  18. 18Or skip the flambé and simply add the Cognac as a deglaze.
  19. 19BUILD THE BRAISE: sprinkle 3 tbsp flour over the vegetables and stir 1 min to cook out the raw taste.
  20. 20Pour in the reserved wine SLOWLY while stirring, scraping the bottom to lift the fond.
  21. 21Add 200 ml beef stock + the bouquet garni + 1.5 tsp salt + 1 tsp pepper.
  22. 22Return the beef + lardons to the pot — the liquid should barely cover everything.
  23. 23SLOW BRAISE: bring to a gentle simmer.
  24. 24Cover with the lid slightly ajar and transfer to a 160 °C oven (or simmer on the stovetop on the lowest heat) for 2.5–3 hours, stirring every 45 min, until the beef is fall-apart tender and the sauce has thickened to a glossy coats-the-spoon richness.
  25. 25Discard the bouquet garni.
  26. 26PEARL ONIONS + MUSHROOMS GARNISH: in a separate skillet, melt 15 g butter over medium heat.
  27. 27Add 250 g peeled pearl onions + 1 tbsp water + 1 tsp sugar + a pinch of salt, cover and cook 15 min, then uncover and brown 5 min until glossy and golden.
  28. 28In the same skillet, add another 15 g butter and 250 g mushrooms, sauté 6–8 min in 2 batches until deeply golden brown — never crowd the pan.
  29. 29ASSEMBLE AND SERVE: fold the pearl onions + mushrooms into the braise.
  30. 30Warm together 5 min.
  31. 31Taste and adjust salt.
  32. 32Let the braise rest 10 min off the heat — the flavours deepen dramatically.
  33. 33Serve generous portions in deep plates over boiled new potatoes (or buttered egg noodles), with crusty baguette to mop up the sauce, scattered with chopped fresh parsley.
  34. 34ALWAYS better the next day — the flavours marry overnight.
  35. 35Drink a bottle of the same Burgundy with which you cooked.
💡
Tip: Let the meat rest for 5 minutes after cooking before slicing or serving.
