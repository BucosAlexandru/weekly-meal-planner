Kimbap

Kimbap — literally seaweed rice — is South Korea's national snack and the perfect food for picnics, long journeys, or a quick lunch. Sesame-oil seasoned rice is wrapped with various fillings (carrot, spinach, eggs, sausage, danmuji, cheese) in a nori sheet and sliced into rounds. It resembles sushi visually but has no vinegar in the rice and a completely different flavor profile. Kimbap emerged in colonial Korea (20th century) and quickly evolved into its own form. Today there are hundreds of variants: tuna kimbap, kimchi kimbap, bulgogi beef kimbap. Kimbap restaurants appear on every street in Korea and offer the best value in Korean cuisine.

Ingredients 4 servings

  • 360 g (2 cups) short-grain white rice, uncooked — makes ~4 rolls
  • 540 ml (2¼ cups) cold water for cooking the rice
  • 4 sheets toasted nori (gim, 20 × 18 cm)
  • 4 strips danmuji (yellow pickled radish), cut to nori length, ~1 cm thick
  • 100 g baby spinach (or 1 small bunch regular spinach)
  • 2 medium carrots (~150 g), julienned 0.5 × 0.5 × 8 cm matchsticks
  • 3 large eggs
  • 200 g imitation crab sticks (or cooked ham, or tuna mixed with mayo)
  • 100 g cucumber (1 small Korean cucumber or 1/3 English cucumber), seeded and cut into long batons
  • 3 tbsp toasted sesame oil (1.5 tbsp for the rice, 0.5 tsp for spinach, the rest for the outer rub)
  • 1.5 tsp fine salt (1 tsp for the rice, pinches for veg and eggs)
  • 2 tsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil for cooking the eggs and carrots
  • 0.5 tsp ground white pepper
  • Bamboo rolling mat (gimbal) + plastic wrap

How to make it

  1. 1RICE: rinse 360 g rice in cold water 3–4 times until the water runs clear.
  2. 2Drain well, then put in a heavy pot with 540 ml fresh cold water.
  3. 3Bring to a boil uncovered over medium-high heat (3–4 min), then cover, reduce to the lowest possible heat and cook 12 minutes — do not lift the lid.
  4. 4Remove from heat and rest covered 10 minutes.
  5. 5The rice should be tender, slightly sticky, with separate grains.
  6. 6SEASON THE RICE: while still hot, transfer to a wide bowl.
  7. 7Drizzle 1.5 tbsp sesame oil, 1 tsp salt and 2 tsp toasted sesame seeds over the top.
  8. 8Fold gently with a rice paddle in cutting motions — NEVER stir aggressively, or the grains break and turn mushy.
  9. 9Cover with a damp cloth and let cool to lukewarm (10–15 min).
  10. 10SPINACH: bring a small pot of salted water to boil.
  11. 11Blanch 100 g spinach 30 seconds until just wilted.
  12. 12Drain and rinse under cold water immediately to stop cooking and preserve the bright green colour.
  13. 13Squeeze in your fist as hard as possible to remove all water — wet spinach ruins the roll.
  14. 14Season with 0.5 tsp sesame oil and a pinch of salt.
  15. 15CARROTS: heat 1 tsp neutral oil in a small pan over medium heat.
  16. 16Add 150 g julienned carrot and a pinch of salt, stir-fry 2 minutes until just softened but still crisp.
  17. 17Tip onto a plate to cool — limp, oily carrots will break the roll.
  18. 18EGGS: beat 3 eggs with a pinch of salt and 0.5 tsp white pepper.
  19. 19Heat 1 tsp neutral oil in a non-stick pan over medium-low heat, pour in the eggs and tilt to form a thin even sheet 25 × 20 cm.
  20. 20Cook 2 minutes until set on top, do not flip.
  21. 21Slide onto a board, let cool 2 minutes, then cut into 4 long strips the width of the nori.
  22. 22PREP THE FILLINGS: cut 100 g cucumber lengthwise into 8 long batons, scrape the seedy core out with a teaspoon.
  23. 23Split 200 g crab sticks in half lengthwise.
  24. 24Cut 4 strips of danmuji to nori length.
  25. 25Arrange everything on a tray in the order you'll lay them: spinach → carrot → cucumber → crab → egg → danmuji.
  26. 26ROLL EACH KIMBAP: place the bamboo mat with the slats running horizontally; lay a sheet of nori shiny-side down.
  27. 27With wet fingers, spread ~150 g rice in a thin even layer over the bottom 3/4 of the nori, leaving a clean 4 cm strip at the far edge.
  28. 28About 3 cm in from the near edge, line up one strip each of spinach, carrot, cucumber, crab, egg and danmuji in a tight bundle.
  29. 29Lift the near edge of the mat and roll firmly forward, tucking the fillings under the rice with the first roll, then continuing to roll tightly to the far edge.
  30. 30Press along the seam to seal the bare nori.
  31. 31CUT AND SERVE: brush the outside of each roll lightly with 0.5 tsp sesame oil to make the nori glossy and prevent it from sticking to the knife.
  32. 32Wipe a sharp knife with a damp cloth between cuts.
  33. 33Slice each roll into 8 even rounds with one decisive push (no sawing) — 32 pieces total.
  34. 34Arrange on a plate, sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  35. 35Eat within 4 hours of rolling; never refrigerate kimbap (the rice goes hard and the nori turns soggy when warmed back up).
💡
Tip: Taste before serving and adjust seasoning and salt to your liking.
